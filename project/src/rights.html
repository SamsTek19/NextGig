Labor Laws & Your Rights

üìã Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA)

  • Minimum Wage: Federal minimum wage is $7.25/hour (states may have higher rates)
  • Overtime Pay: 1.5x regular pay for hours worked over 40 per week
  • Child Labor: Restrictions on work hours and types of work for minors
  • Record Keeping: Employers must maintain accurate time and pay records

üè• Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA)

  • Up to 12 weeks of unpaid leave for qualifying medical and family reasons
  • Job protection during leave period
  • Continuation of health insurance benefits
  • Applies to employers with 50+ employees

üõ°Ô∏è Occupational Safety and Health Act (OSHA)

  • Right to a safe and healthy workplace
  • Right to report safety concerns without retaliation
  • Right to request workplace safety inspections
  • Right to access safety training and information

Workplace Safety & Health

üö® Your Safety Rights

  • Safe Equipment: Right to properly maintained and safe tools/equipment
  • Safety Training: Right to receive proper safety training for your job
  • Protective Equipment: Right to appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE)
  • Hazard Communication: Right to know about workplace hazards and chemicals

üìû Reporting Safety Issues

  • Report safety concerns to your supervisor immediately
  • Contact OSHA if employer doesn't address concerns
  • File a complaint online at osha.gov or call 1-800-321-OSHA
  • You're protected from retaliation for reporting safety issues

üè• Workers' Compensation

  • Medical benefits for work-related injuries/illnesses
  • Lost wages compensation during recovery
  • Rehabilitation services if needed
  • File a claim immediately after workplace injury

Fair Pay & Benefits

üí∞ Minimum Wage Laws

  • Federal Minimum: $7.25/hour (as of 2024)
  • State Minimums: Many states have higher minimum wages
  • Local Minimums: Some cities have even higher rates
  • Tip Workers: Minimum $2.13/hour if tips bring total to at least minimum wage

‚è∞ Overtime Pay

  • 1.5x regular pay for hours over 40 per week
  • Some states require overtime for 8+ hours per day
  • Exempt employees (salaried) may not qualify for overtime
  • Keep accurate records of all hours worked

üíº Benefits & Protections

  • Health Insurance: May be required for employers with 50+ employees
  • Unemployment: Available if you lose your job through no fault of your own
  • Social Security: Contributions made through payroll taxes
  • Equal Pay: Same pay for same work regardless of gender, race, etc.

Discrimination & Harassment Protection

üö´ Protected Characteristics

  • Race & Color: Protection from racial discrimination
  • Gender & Sex: Equal treatment regardless of gender
  • Age: Protection for workers 40 and older
  • Disability: Reasonable accommodations required
  • Religion: Accommodation for religious practices
  • National Origin: Protection regardless of country of origin

üö® Harassment Prevention

  • Sexual harassment is illegal in all workplaces
  • Verbal, physical, and visual harassment are prohibited
  • Employers must investigate harassment complaints
  • Retaliation for reporting harassment is illegal

üìû Reporting Discrimination

  • Report to your supervisor or HR department first
  • File a complaint with the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC)
  • Contact EEOC at +233-551-694-282 or eeoc.gov
  • File within 180 days of the discriminatory act
  • Consider consulting with an employment attorney
